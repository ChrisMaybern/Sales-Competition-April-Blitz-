03 — ICP Qualification

Who Qualifies

Private Equity GP  ·  Closed-End Fund Structures  ·  U.S.-Domiciled  ·  $1B+ AUM

✓ Qualifies
  • Private equity GP managing closed-end funds
  • U.S.-domiciled firm (HQ or primary GP operations)
  • $1B+ AUM at the firm level — not fund level
  • Real estate PE with closed-end fund vehicles
✕ Does Not Qualify
  • Venture capital — excluded regardless of AUM
  • Open-end / evergreen fund structures
  • Hedge funds (no closed-end waterfall)
  • Non-U.S. domiciled firms
  • Fund administrators or service providers
  • Firms below $1B AUM at firm level
  • Family offices (unless structured as PE GP with closed-end funds)

All 4 criteria must be met. Three out of four does not qualify. When in doubt on ICP fit — ask Christopher before you book, not after.

04 — Qualified Personas

Who You Need to Book

✓ Qualifies — 1 Point Each
  • Controller / Fund Controller
  • VP of Finance / VP of Fund Accounting
  • CFO / Head of Finance
  • COO / Head of Operations
  • Any role that clearly owns fund calculation, waterfall governance, or finance leadership — function beats title
✕ Does Not Qualify — 0 Points
  • Investor Relations — explicitly excluded
  • LP Communications / Head of IR / VP of IR
  • Marketing, Business Development
  • Portfolio company contacts
  • Anyone who doesn't own the fund calculation or reporting workflow
IR — The Only Two Exceptions (IR Still Scores 0)
✓ Exception 1 — On the Same Call

IR brings a qualified contact (Controller, VP Finance, CFO, COO) onto the same meeting. That person scores 1 point. IR still scores 0.

✓ Exception 2 — Direct Referral

IR directly refers you and you book a separate formal meeting before April 30, scheduled by May 14. That meeting scores 1 point. IR still scores 0.

A warm email intro is not a booked meeting. "I'll mention you to our CFO" does not count.

05 — Meeting Definitions

What Counts

All 5 Conditions Must Be Met
  • Booked (calendar invite sent) during April 1–30
  • Scheduled to take place on or before May 14
  • Accepted by the prospect before May 14
  • Prospect firm passes ICP qualification
  • Contact is a qualified persona (not IR unless converted)
  • Logged in Salesforce within 24 hrs of booking
  • Official meeting type: Initial Call, Demo, etc. — not a catch-up or coffee
Disqualified
  • Booked before April 1 or after April 30
  • Scheduled after May 14 or not accepted by May 14
  • Firm has existing open pipeline in Salesforce
  • Existing Maybern customer
  • IR meeting that doesn't convert
  • "Catch-up," "coffee," or any informal meeting
  • Inbound lead with no April outreach triggering it
  • Marketing-generated inbound — never counts
  • Sandbagged meetings from March
  • Duplicate with same contact at same firm

Salesforce = source of truth. Not logged = doesn't count. Log every meeting within 24 hrs of booking.

Full window: Booked Apr 1–30 · Scheduled by May 14 · Accepted by May 14 · Winner declared May 16.

09 — Ground Rules

Expectations & Fair Play

Required
  • Book only ICP-qualified firms and qualified personas
  • Log all meetings in Salesforce within 24 hrs of booking
  • Use official meeting types only — Initial Call, Demo, etc.
  • AE partners coordinate to avoid doubling up on same accounts
  • First to book a contact gets credit — speed wins
  • Reschedules within the window are fine — no-shows don't disqualify
  • When unsure on ICP fit — ask Christopher before booking
Prohibited
  • No sandbagging meetings from March into April
  • No booking firms with existing open pipeline in Salesforce
  • No "catch-up," "coffee," or informal meetings
  • No marketing-generated or passive inbounds
  • No meetings with existing Maybern customers
  • No duplicate meetings with the same contact at the same firm
  • No gaming the window by canceling/rescheduling outside it

Zero Tolerance — Collusion: No AE may direct, instruct, or arrange with an SDR to prospect specific accounts on their behalf. Violation = immediate AE team disqualification. SDR keeps their points — consequence falls entirely on the AE.

11 — Edge Cases

The One Rule That Covers Everything Else

Anything not explicitly covered in the rules or FAQ is subject to Christopher's sole discretion. All rulings will be made fairly, objectively, and with one guiding question:

"Does this align with the spirit of the competition?"

Good faith activity that builds real qualified pipeline will always be viewed favorably — even if the rules don't explicitly cover the scenario.

Gaming the structure, inflating numbers, or circumventing the intent of the rules will not — regardless of technical compliance.

Christopher's ruling on any uncovered edge case is final. Rulings communicated to the full team when relevant. When in doubt — ask before you act, not after.
