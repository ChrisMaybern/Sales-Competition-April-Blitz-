General Rules
7 questions
G1How do the two competitions relate to each other?+
Fully Separate — No Crossover

The SDR and AE competitions are completely independent. SDR meetings do not contribute to AE team totals — not for points, not as a tiebreaker, not in any scenario. AE-sourced meetings do not affect SDR counts. The two competitions run in parallel and share only the ICP criteria, booking window, and acceptance deadline.

G2What is the booking window exactly?+
April 1–30 · 22 Business Days

The calendar invite must be sent during April 1–30. The meeting must also be scheduled to take place on or before May 14 and accepted by the prospect before May 14. April starts on a Wednesday, giving you 22 business days.

✓ Valid: Meeting booked April 22, scheduled for May 10, accepted April 25. Counts.
✕ Invalid: Meeting booked March 28 for an April date. Outside the booking window.
✕ Invalid: Meeting booked April 28, scheduled for May 20. Scheduled date is after May 14 — excluded.
G3Does the meeting itself have to take place before April 30?+
No — But Must Be Scheduled By May 14

The meeting does not have to take place in April, but it must be scheduled (on the calendar) for no later than May 14. Booking in April for a May 20 meeting does not count — the scheduled date must fall within the window.

✓ Valid window: Booked April 1–30 · Scheduled May 1–14 · Accepted before May 14.
G4What happens if a prospect cancels after May 14?+
Does Not Count

The meeting must be accepted and scheduled before May 14. An outright cancellation after the deadline removes it from the count. A reschedule to a date that still falls on or before May 14 where the prospect remains committed is fine — a reschedule pushed past May 14 is treated the same as a cancellation.

G5What if the prospect no-shows but wants to reschedule?+
Counts — Reschedule Is Still the Same Meeting

A no-show followed by a reschedule still counts as the same M1, as long as the rescheduled meeting gets accepted by May 14. The original booking date is what matters. Reps should not be penalized for a prospect's no-show.

✕ Does not count: Prospect no-shows, reschedules, but never formally accepts the new time by May 14.
G6Who resolves disputes at payout?+
Christopher & Riley — Final Decision

Christopher and Riley validate all meetings for ICP and persona fit before bonuses are processed on May 16. Disputes are resolved within 48 hours. Their decision is final. When in doubt about whether something qualifies — ask before you book, not at payout.

G7What if a prospect reaches out to us — does an inbound meeting count?+
Default: No — Inbounds Do Not Count

If a prospect contacts Maybern on their own initiative with no prior outreach from the rep during the competition window, the meeting does not count. Passive inbounds are not pipeline building — they're order taking.

The one exception — outreach-triggered inbound: If a rep can demonstrate that their own outreach sent during April directly prompted the prospect to respond inbound, the meeting may count. This is reviewed case-by-case by Christopher at payout. The rep must be able to show the Salesforce activity — email, call log, LinkedIn message — with a timestamp that connects the April outreach to the inbound response.

✕ Marketing-generated inbound: Prospect responds to a Maybern email campaign, webinar, or any marketing activity. Never counts — no exceptions.
✕ Pre-window outreach: Rep reached out in March (or earlier) and the prospect responds in April. Does not count. The outreach that triggered the inbound must have been sent during April.
✓ Outreach-triggered inbound: Rep sends a cold email on April 8. Prospect replies April 10 saying they're interested and wants to meet. Rep books the meeting. Documented in Salesforce. May count — subject to Christopher's review at payout.
If a prospect responds to another rep's outreach and a different rep books the meeting — the rep who booked it gets the credit. Extreme edge cases at Christopher's discretion.

ICP & Firm Eligibility
8 questions
I1What is the ICP for this competition?+
4 Requirements — All Must Be Met
1. Private equity GP managing closed-end fund structures
2. U.S.-domiciled — HQ or primary operations in the U.S.
3. $1B+ AUM at the firm level (not individual fund level)
4. Closed-end fund vehicles — not evergreen or open-end

All four must be true. Three out of four does not qualify.

I2Does venture capital qualify?+
Never — VC Is Explicitly Excluded

Venture capital is disqualified regardless of AUM. A $10B VC firm does not qualify. Maybern solves closed-end waterfall calculation — VC doesn't run that structure. There are no exceptions to this rule.

✕ Does not qualify: Any firm whose primary fund strategy is venture, early-stage, or seed — regardless of size.
I3Do open-end or evergreen fund structures qualify?+
No — Closed-End Only

Open-end and evergreen structures are disqualified. This includes hedge funds, which have no closed-end waterfall. The product requires closed-end fund mechanics to be relevant.

I4What if the firm is primarily VC but has a small PE or closed-end arm?+
Does Not Qualify

If the firm's primary structure is VC, it doesn't qualify — even with a PE sleeve. The firm's dominant fund strategy must be closed-end PE. A VC firm with a side buyout vehicle is still a VC firm.

✓ Qualifies: PE/buyout firm whose primary program is closed-end funds at $1B+ AUM.
✕ Does not qualify: VC firm with $6B AUM that recently launched its first small buyout vehicle.
I5The firm is foreign-headquartered but has a significant U.S. office — does it qualify?+
Only If Primary GP Entity Is U.S.-Based

U.S.-domiciled means the GP entity is incorporated or primarily operating in the U.S. A U.S. satellite office of a London-headquartered GP does not qualify. If the primary GP entity and fund management operations are U.S.-based, it qualifies even with international offices.

When in doubt, check with Christopher before booking. Don't assume — U.S. domicile applies to the GP entity, not where individual partners happen to sit.
I6What if the firm's AUM isn't publicly confirmed?+
Yes — With a Documented Source

You don't need an audited figure. Use Preqin, PitchBook, the firm's own website, or press releases. Log the source in Salesforce. If it gets challenged at ICP review, you need to show where the number came from.

If you genuinely can't confirm $1B+ from any available source, ask Christopher first. Don't book and hope.
I7Same parent firm, different fund entity with a separate finance team — do both count?+
Yes — If Separate Entity, Separate Finance Team, No Open Pipeline

Different fund entities with genuinely independent finance teams are treated as separate prospects. A meeting with Entity B's Controller counts even if there's existing pipeline at Entity A — as long as Entity B has no active Salesforce opportunity.

I8What about family offices?+
Only If Structured as a Genuine PE GP

Family offices are excluded unless they operate as a genuine PE GP with closed-end fund vehicles, LP relationships, and waterfall distributions at $1B+ AUM. A family office that simply manages a portfolio does not qualify.

Personas & IR
7 questions
P1Who are the qualified personas?+
Qualifies or Doesn't — No Tiers
✓ Qualifies (1 pt each): Controller / Fund Controller · VP of Finance · VP of Fund Accounting · CFO / Head of Finance · COO / Head of Operations · Any role that clearly owns fund calculation, waterfall governance, or finance leadership

✕ Does not qualify (0 pts): Any IR function · LP Communications · Marketing · Business Development · Anyone who doesn't own the fund calculation or reporting workflow

It's binary — a contact either owns or directly influences the fund calculation and reporting workflow, or they don't. If they do, they qualify. If they don't, they don't count.

P2The title doesn't exactly match — how do I know if the contact qualifies?+
Function Wins Over Title

If the person's actual responsibilities clearly match a Tier 1 or Tier 2 function — fund accounting oversight, waterfall governance, finance leadership — they qualify regardless of exact title. Christopher and Riley make the call at ICP review.

✓ Likely qualifies: "Director of Fund Finance" · "Head of Accounting" · "Managing Director, Finance" — function maps to fund calculation or finance leadership
✕ Does not qualify: "Director of Investor Relations" · "Head of LP Communications" · Any IR function regardless of title
Ambiguous title? Slack Christopher before booking. A disqualified meeting can drop you below the bonus threshold.
P3Does Investor Relations — or any other non-qualified title — count?+
No — 0 Points

IR and any other non-qualified title earns 0 points. This is the same rule for every non-qualified contact — there are no special IR pathways, retroactive credits, or referral mechanics. If the person on the call doesn't own the fund calculation or reporting workflow, the meeting scores 0.

Do not book meetings with non-qualified titles expecting them to count. They will be removed at ICP review.
P4Is there any exception for non-qualified titles?+
One Exception — Qualified Persona on the Same Call

If a non-qualified contact brings a qualified persona (Controller, VP Finance, CFO, COO) onto the same call, that qualified person scores 1 point. The non-qualified contact still scores 0.

✓ Counts: IR brings the CFO onto the call → CFO scores 1 point. IR scores 0.
✕ Does not count: IR meeting with no qualified persona present → 0 points, full stop.

Retroactive credit edge case: If you book a meeting with a non-qualified contact and that contact subsequently facilitates a separate qualified meeting at the same firm — booked before April 30 and scheduled by May 14 — the original non-qualified meeting retroactively earns 1 point. This is validated by Christopher at payout. The connection between the two meetings must be genuine and demonstrable.

Example: You book IR on April 5. IR personally introduces you to the Controller. You book the Controller meeting April 12. At payout, Christopher confirms the connection — IR retroactively earns 1pt, Controller meeting earns 1pt = 2 points total.
Does not qualify: IR mentions a name, you cold-email that person independently and book them. The IR meeting did not facilitate the booking — no retroactive credit.
Retroactive credit is validated at payout, not during the month. When in doubt, document the connection — Slack Christopher with context so it's on record before April 30.
P5You book a CFO meeting and the Controller shows up too — do both count?+
One Calendar Event = One Point

One meeting equals one point regardless of how many qualified people attend. CFO + Controller on the same call = 1 point. If you want to count the Controller separately, book them their own dedicated meeting.

P6What if a contact gets promoted during April — does their tier change?+
Qualification Status Locked at Time of Booking

Whether a contact qualifies is determined at the time the calendar invite is sent. If you booked an IR contact who subsequently gets promoted to CFO, the meeting was booked as an IR meeting and IR rules apply. No retroactive upgrades based on title changes after booking.

P7Can you book multiple contacts at the same firm and have each count separately?+
Yes — Different Contacts Count Separately

Booking the Controller and the CFO at the same firm = 2 points. Each qualified contact at a qualifying firm is a separate credit, as long as they're genuinely different stakeholders in separate calendar events.

✕ Does not count twice: Booking the same person twice at the same firm. One contact, one credit.

Meeting Types
5 questions
MT1What meeting types count for the Blitz?+
Official Sales Stages Only

Meetings must align with official Maybern sales methodology stages and be logged against a formal Salesforce activity type.

✓ Count: Initial Call (M1) · Initial Demo (M2) · Coordination Meeting (M3) · Value Creation (M4) · Tech File Review (M5) · Process Review (M6) · and subsequent official stage meetings
✕ Do not count: "Catch-up" · "Coffee" · "Quick sync" · "Reconnect" · "Informal intro" · Any meeting not logged against a formal Salesforce activity type
MT2Can a rep change the meeting type after the fact to claim more points?+
No — Meeting Type Locked at Booking

The Salesforce activity type logged at time of booking is what counts. Retroactively changing a meeting type is not allowed and will be flagged at ICP review. If you ran a long Initial Call that covered demo content, it's still 1 point.

MT3What if one call covers both M1 and M2 content in a single session?+
One Event = One Point

You cannot claim two meetings from one calendar event. One 90-minute session covering Initial Call and Initial Demo content = 1 point. Book two separate meetings if you want two credits.

MT4Do SDRs book M2+ meetings?+
No — SDRs Book M1s Only

SDRs are responsible for booking Initial Calls (M1) only. The 1.5x momentum multiplier is an AE-only mechanic. SDRs earn 1 point per qualified M1 booked — that's the full scope of the SDR competition.

MT5What qualifies as a legitimate M2 for the AE multiplier?+
Genuine Next Step — Not a Rebook

An M2 must represent a genuine progression in the sales cycle — an Initial Demo, a Coordination Meeting, a deeper discovery session with additional stakeholders. The original M1 must have taken place or been genuinely accepted before M2 earns the multiplier.

✕ Not an M2: Rebooking the same Initial Call because the prospect no-showed. That's still an M1 rescheduled.
✕ Will be flagged: Booking M1 and M2 on the same day before M1 has occurred. M2 must be a genuine next step, not a same-day double-book.

Territory & Assignment
5 questions
T1Can SDRs prospect accounts that aren't assigned to any AE?+
Yes — Unassigned Territory Is Fair Game

SDRs can and should prospect unassigned territory. If an SDR books a qualified meeting at an unassigned account, the account is assigned to an AE based on geography. For New York-based reps, assignment follows a round robin rotation. The SDR's point counts regardless of how assignment resolves.

T2What if an SDR books into an AE's territory that hasn't been touched in months?+
Fair Game — If No Open Pipeline

If there is no active opportunity in Salesforce, the account is eligible regardless of territory assignment. Stale territory is unprotected territory. This is a natural forcing function for AEs to keep Salesforce current.

AEs: if an account matters to you, keep it active. An SDR booking into a dormant account is not a violation — it's a gap you left open.
T3Two SDRs prospect the same account simultaneously — who gets credit?+
First Calendar Invite Sent Wins

The SDR who sends the calendar invite first gets the credit. The duplicate is removed. This is auditable via Salesforce timestamp — no gray area. Speed wins.

T4What if the assigned AE refuses to run a meeting the SDR booked?+
SDR's Point Stands — AE Issue Is a Management Matter

The SDR did their job. Their point counts regardless. Christopher will assign a replacement AE if possible. AE non-cooperation is a coaching issue — it does not penalize the SDR.

T5What if the AE is on PTO when the meeting happens?+
AE's Responsibility to Manage

It's the AE's responsibility to reschedule within the window or arrange coverage before going on PTO. If the meeting falls outside the window as a result, it doesn't count. Reps own their calendar management.

Existing Pipeline
5 questions
PL1What if there's an open opportunity in Salesforce but nobody's touched it in 6 months?+
Ineligible While the Opportunity Is Open

If there's an open opportunity in Salesforce — regardless of last activity date — the account is off limits. The rule is simple: open opp = ineligible. Formally close-lose dead opportunities before April 1 if you want the account to be eligible.

Use this as a forcing function. Clean up your pipeline before April 1. Close-lose dead opps. It helps forecast accuracy and frees accounts for the Blitz.
PL2What about accounts with a closed-lost opportunity from last year?+
Fully Eligible — Closed-Lost Is Fair Game

Closed-lost means the opportunity is gone. A new meeting at a previously lost account is legitimate new pipeline activity and absolutely counts. These are often the best accounts to target — they already understand the problem space.

✓ Pro tip: Pull your closed-lost accounts from the last 12–18 months. That's a ready-made list for April 1.
PL3What if it's an existing Maybern customer?+
Never Counts

Meetings with existing Maybern customers are excluded from both competitions. This is about new pipeline — not expansion or customer success activity.

PL4An existing customer refers you to a sister firm — does that count?+
Yes — If ICP Qualifies and No Open Pipeline

A referral to a separate firm is a new lead. As long as the referred firm independently passes ICP and has no active Salesforce opportunity, it's eligible. Customer referrals are some of the best leads — this behavior should be rewarded.

PL5What if I sandbagged meetings from March to book in April?+
Disqualified — Immediate Removal

Meetings that were already in motion or committed before April 1 are excluded even if the calendar invite is technically sent on April 1 or later. Christopher and Riley will review for sandbagging at payout. This will be caught.

Points & Scoring
8 questions
S1How does the AE momentum multiplier work?+
M1 = 1pt · M2 = 1.5pts · M3+ = 2.25pts

AEs earn 1 point for each M1 they personally book. M2 follow-ups earn 1.5 points. M3 and beyond earn 2.25 points each. The multiplier escalates to reward depth — advancing a deal beats spraying first calls.

AE self-sources M1 + M2 + M3: 1.0 + 1.5 + 2.25 = 4.75 points from one prospect.
vs. 4 separate M1s: 4.0 points. Going deep beats going wide.
S2SDR books M1, AE books M2 — what does each person score?+
SDR: 1pt · AE: 1.5pts on M2 · AE: 2.25pts on M3

SDR books M1 = 1 point for the SDR (SDR competition only). AE books M2 = 1.5 points for the AE. AE books M3 = 2.25 points. No shared credit, no crossover between competitions.

Full sequence: SDR books M1 (SDR +1pt) → AE books M2 (AE +1.5pts) → AE books M3 (AE +2.25pts). SDR total = 1pt. AE total = 3.75pts.
S3Do SDR meetings contribute to the AE team total in any way?+
Never — Not Even in a Tiebreaker

SDR-sourced meetings do not feed AE team totals under any circumstances — not for points, not as a tiebreaker, not in any scenario. AE team totals are built exclusively from meetings the AEs personally book.

S4One AE books an M1, a different AE books M2 — does the second AE get the 1.5x multiplier?+
Yes — Multiplier Follows the Booker of the Follow-Up

The multiplier applies to whoever books the follow-up meeting, regardless of who booked the prior meeting. If the M1 genuinely occurred and the M2 is a legitimate next step, the rep who booked M2 earns 1.5pts. This is the same logic as the SDR → AE handoff.

Asaf books M1 (Team 2: +1pt) → Gabi books M2 (Team 1: +1.5pts): Each rep earns credit for what they personally booked. The multiplier is Gabi's because she booked the follow-up.
The M1 must have actually occurred before M2 earns the multiplier. You cannot same-day book M1 and M2 at a firm you've never contacted. Manufactured sequences will be flagged at ICP review.
S5One AE gives another AE a referral — who gets the points?+
Points Go to Whoever Books the Meeting

A referral from a teammate is just a lead. The rep who books the meeting gets the credit — not the rep who passed the name. If Asaf gives Gabi a referral and Gabi books the M1, Gabi gets 1pt and Asaf gets 0.

Asaf refers a firm to Gabi → Gabi books M1: Gabi +1pt. Asaf +0.
Asaf refers a firm → Gabi books M1 → Gabi books M2: Gabi gets 1pt (M1) + 1.5pts (M2) = 2.5pts. Asaf gets 0.
This applies across teams and within the same team. The booker owns the credit, always.
S6Two AE teammates both book meetings at the same firm with different contacts — do both count?+
Yes — Different Contacts Count Separately

Partner A books the CFO and Partner B books the Controller at the same firm = 2 points toward the team total. This is exactly the kind of multi-threaded outreach you want to encourage. Coordinate to make sure you're targeting genuinely different stakeholders.

First to book a specific contact gets credit. If both partners accidentally book the same person at the same firm, the duplicate is removed.
S7How do fractional points work?+
Round Down for SDR · Count As-Is for AE Teams

For SDR bonus payouts, the total point count is rounded down to the nearest whole number before checking the threshold and calculating the payout increment. For AE team competition totals, fractional points count as-is — 10.5 points beats 10.0.

S8What is the AE tiebreaker?+
3-Step Ladder
Step 1: Most total qualified meetings. Still tied? →
Step 2: Most total opportunity amount created during the window. Still tied? →
Step 3: $6,000 splits evenly — $1,500 per person across both teams.

No other tiebreaker metrics apply. AUM tiers, geography, and any other criteria are not used.

SDR Bonus Rules
4 questions
SDR1I hit 9 meetings but 2 get disqualified at ICP review — I'm now at 7. Do I lose the whole bonus?+
Yes — Below Gate, No Bonus

7 is below the 8-meeting threshold. The bonus does not activate. This is the harshest outcome in the competition and it's intentional — the gate exists to enforce quality, not just volume.

The most important rule to internalize before April 1: Do not book gray-area accounts hoping they'll pass ICP review. One disqualified meeting at 8 or 9 can eliminate your entire bonus. When in doubt — ask Christopher before you book, not after.
SDR2I hit the threshold but I'm leaving the company before May 16 payout — do I still get paid?+
Yes — Earned Comp Is Paid

You earned it in April, you get paid on May 16 regardless of employment status at payout date. The work was done and the results are validated. Earned compensation is not forfeited due to departure timing.

SDR3I have 13 meetings — do I get credit for the extra 3 above the 10?+
No — Increments of 5 Only, No Partial Credit

Bonuses pay in clean increments of 5. 13 meetings pays like 10 ($2,000). The extra 3 don't roll forward or pro-rate. Push to 15 to hit the next $1,000 increment.

Full payout table: 1–7 = $0 · 8–9 = $1,000 · 10–14 = $2,000 · 15–19 = $3,000 · 20–24 = $4,000 · 25+ = +$1K per 5
SDR4Is there a cap on SDR earnings?+
No Cap

There is no ceiling on the SDR individual bonus. Every 5 qualified meetings above the threshold adds $1,000. 25 meetings = $5,000. 30 = $6,000. Book clean, book fast, keep going.

AE Team Rules
5 questions
AE1How does the 65/35 balance rule work?+
Neither Partner Can Exceed 65% of Team Total

If one partner accounts for more than 65% of the team's total qualified meetings, the team is disqualified — regardless of total count. Both partners must contribute meaningfully.

Team books 10: 6/4 (60/40) ✓ · 7/3 (70/30) ✕ disqualified
Team books 12: 7/5 (58/42) ✓ · 8/4 (67/33) ✕ disqualified
Team books 8: 5/3 (62.5/37.5) ✓
Check your split at the April 15 leaderboard. If one partner is over 65%, you have two weeks to rebalance.
AE2One AE on a team has PTO for two weeks — is there any accommodation to the 65/35 rule?+
No Accommodation

Teams are assigned knowing the full month of April is the window. Planned PTO needs to be front-loaded or discussed with Christopher before the competition starts. The 65/35 rule doesn't flex for scheduled absences.

AE3What if an AE has a genuine personal emergency mid-competition?+
Case-by-Case — Christopher Decides

A genuine unforeseen emergency is handled by Christopher on a case-by-case basis with compassion. This is not in the written rules — it's a judgment call reserved for true emergencies. Planned absences and scheduling inconveniences do not qualify.

AE4Both AE partners accidentally prospect the same account — what happens?+
First to Book Gets Credit — Duplicate Removed

The first calendar invite sent gets the credit. The duplicate is removed from the count. This is why AE partners need to coordinate outreach — doubling up wastes effort and costs your team a point.

AE5An AE directs or asks an SDR to prospect specific accounts for them — what happens?+
AE Team Disqualified — Zero Tolerance

No AE may direct, instruct, or arrange with an SDR to prospect specific accounts on their behalf. Any such arrangement results in immediate disqualification of the AE team. The SDR keeps all their points. The consequence falls entirely on the AE.

This includes: asking an SDR to "focus on" certain accounts, coaching SDR outreach to benefit your AE territory, or any arrangement where the SDR's prospecting is shaped by AE direction rather than genuine ICP fit.
SDRs: if an AE pressures you to prospect on their behalf, flag it to Christopher immediately. You will not be penalized.

Tracking & Payout
4 questions
TR1What's the Salesforce logging requirement?+
Log Within 24 Hours

Every meeting must be logged in Salesforce within 24 hours of booking. Meetings logged after May 14 are excluded entirely. Salesforce is the source of truth — if it's not logged, it doesn't exist for Blitz purposes.

Log every meeting the day you book it. Don't batch-log at the end of the week. A missed log can cost you a point you legitimately earned.
TR2How does the leaderboard work?+
Weekly in Slack · Pending Until Accepted

The leaderboard is updated weekly in Slack. SDR individual counts and all 3 AE team standings are visible to the full team. Booked meetings show as "pending" until the prospect accepts. A mid-month check-in is published on April 15 showing standings at the halfway point.

TR3When are bonuses processed and how are disputes handled?+
May 16 · Disputes Within 48 Hours

Final count and ICP review is completed on May 16. Bonuses are announced and processed that day. If you believe a meeting was incorrectly excluded, disputes are resolved within 48 hours. Christopher and Riley's decision is final.

TR4Can rules change mid-competition?+
Only With 48 Hours Notice — Never Retroactive

Christopher reserves the right to adjust rules with 48 hours notice if an unforeseen edge case arises that isn't covered here. Any change will be communicated to the full team simultaneously and will apply from the date of notice forward — never retroactively.

TR5What happens if something comes up that isn't covered anywhere in this FAQ?+
Christopher's Discretion — Final and Binding

Any edge case, scenario, or situation not explicitly addressed in this document is subject to Christopher's sole discretion. All rulings will be made fairly and objectively, with one guiding principle: does this align with the spirit of the competition?

The spirit of the competition is: Build real, qualified pipeline with prospects Maybern can genuinely win. Any activity that serves that goal in good faith will be viewed favorably. Any activity that games the structure, inflates numbers, or circumvents the intent of the rules will not.
Christopher's ruling on any uncovered edge case is final. Rulings will be communicated to the full team when relevant so everyone benefits from the clarification. When in doubt — ask before you act, not after.
